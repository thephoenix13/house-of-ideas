01 — Brand & voice

What we are, and how we sound.

Essence

House of Ideas is where India’s sharpest minds build the ventures the country actually needs.

Considered.   Selective.   Builder.

The promise

We bring the idea, the capital, the plan, and the mentorship. The Fellow brings the execution.

Voice
  • Plain over puffed. No “synergistic” anything. No “10x”, no “disruption”.
  • Specific over sweeping. Name the idea, the capital, the plan, the mentorship.
  • Calm over loud. The brand carries authority. It doesn’t shout.
  • First-person plural, sparingly. “We” for the studio; declarative third person otherwise.
Lines that travel
  • “We bring the idea, the capital, the plan, and the mentorship. The Fellow brings the execution.”
  • “We don’t wait for founders to pitch us. We do the upstream work.”
  • “The filter is the differentiation.”
  • “Conviction before capital.”
Naming
House of Ideas / HoICapital H, lowercase o, capital I. Never HOI, never Hoi.
FellowThe operator. Capitalised. Plural: Fellows. The program is the Founders Fellowship.
ventureThe portfolio company. Lowercase. Avoid “startup” in formal copy.
IdeaA researched entry in the catalogue. Capitalised in that sense, lowercase in general use.
FoundersThe product line in scope. Workforce is deferred — do not surface externally.

02 — Logo & wordmark

Wordmark-led. The brand is the name.

No pictorial mark for the primary logo. The wordmark is set in the display serif with generous spacing — masthead energy, not a startup logo.

Primary lockup
House of Ideas
Monogram
HoI HoI For favicons, avatars, and tight spaces. A thin rule-line box reads like a publisher’s seal.

Do

  • Keep the wordmark single-weight, letter-spaced, on ink or paper.
  • Use the monogram only where the full wordmark won’t fit.
  • Give it room — generous clear space on all sides.

Don’t

  • No abstract marks, lightbulbs, brains, or gradients.
  • No app-icon styling competing on a Play Store grid.
  • Don’t set the wordmark in the sans or distort the spacing.

Open: custom-drawn serif vs. off-the-shelf, and the public tagline, are unresolved — see the brand outline §11.

04 — Colour

Ink, paper, and one signal.

The palette is near-black ink on warm paper, with a single ochre accent used sparingly. If two ochre elements share a screen, one is too many. Never use the accent for body text. No gradients, no shadows beyond minimal elevation.

--ink #0E0E0C · text, surfaces
--ink-soft #2A2925 · body copy
--paper #F6F1E8 · background
--paper-soft #FAF5EC · card surfaces
--ochre #B8862F · accent, sparingly
--ochre-deep #9A6F23 · accent hover
--stone #6B6660 · secondary text
--stone-soft #8F8A82 · tertiary text
--rule #D9D2C5 · hairline dividers

Motion

If motion is used at all: slow, considered, eased. Fades and minor positional shifts only. Nothing bounces, nothing spins. Hover the swatch to feel the standard transition.

--ease

All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion — reduce it in your OS and transitions collapse to near-zero.

08 — Do & don’t

The line we hold.

Do

  • Lead with specificity — numbers, names, mechanisms.
  • Let white space and silence do work.
  • Treat the Fellow as the protagonist of every external story.
  • Reference the upstream research as proof of seriousness.

Don’t

  • Use startup clichés — “disrupt”, “unicorn”, “10x”, “rockstar”, “ninja”.
  • Promise outcomes — “guaranteed exit”, “first unicorn from our batch”.
  • Use the IIT/NIT filter to mock other institutions.
  • Use saffron-white-green together — heritage cues stay subtle.
  • Over-explain the model in every piece of copy. Trust the reader.
