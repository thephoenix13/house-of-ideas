# House of Ideas — Social Calendar

**Window:** June – August 2026 (13 weeks) · **Objective:** brand launch & awareness · **Cadence:** moderate

A 3-month plan to introduce House of Ideas from zero — establishing the voice, the positioning, and the five messaging pillars across all audiences. This is the operational companion to the strategic [`brand-outline.md`](brand-outline.md) (voice, audience segments §7, messaging pillars §8) and the [`foundations.html`](../foundations.html) style guide (visual rules).

> **Phase note.** The objective is *awareness*, not open applications — Fellow applications are not confirmed open in this window. CTAs build toward *interest and following*, not a hard application deadline. Adjust if the application window is set.

## 4. The weekly rhythm (repeatable skeleton)

Each week starts from **one anchor idea** (the Monday LinkedIn post). Everything else is a re-cut of it for a different channel and length. This keeps quality high and production low.

| Day | Slot | Derived from |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | LinkedIn — **anchor** (the week's idea, full) | original |
| **Tue** | X — **thread** expanding the anchor | anchor |
| **Tue** | Instagram — **carousel** (visual version of the anchor) | anchor |
| **Wed** | LinkedIn — **secondary** (proof / different pillar) | original |
| **Thu** | X — **short take** (one declarative; reply-baits a real question) | secondary |
| **Fri** | LinkedIn — **lighter** (Idea teaser / human angle) | original |
| **Fri** | Instagram — **quote card** (a line that travels) | anchor or Manifesto |
| **Fri** | X — **one-liner** (the week's sharpest sentence) | anchor |

**Rule of thumb:** write the anchor and the secondary fresh each week (2 original pieces); the other 7 slots are repurposes. The newsletter (biweekly) is its own original long-form.

## 5. Voice & production guardrails

Pulled from the brand. Pin these where the team writes.

- **Calm over loud.** No "excited to announce", no emojis-as-personality, no exclamation stacks.
- **Banned vocabulary:** disrupt, unicorn, 10x, rockstar, ninja, game-changer, revolutionary, ecosystem-builder, synergy.
- **Specific over sweeping.** Name the idea, the capital, the plan, the mentorship. Numbers and mechanisms beat adjectives.
- **The Fellow is the protagonist.** When in doubt, write *to* a student, not *about* HoI.
- **No promised outcomes.** Never "guaranteed exit" or "our first unicorn".
- **The filter is positioning, not snobbery.** Never mock other institutions.
- **Visuals:** ink + paper + ochre *sparingly* (one accent per asset). Typographic editorial cards, not illustration. No saffron-white-green together.

## 10. Measurement

Awareness-phase KPIs — favour *quality* signals over vanity. Review at each month boundary.

| Tier | Metric | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Reach** | Follower growth per platform; post impressions | Are we being seen by the right segments? |
| **Resonance** | Saves + shares + meaningful replies (not just likes) | On-brand content earns saves, not applause |
| **Intent** | Profile visits → site clicks; newsletter signups | Is awareness converting to interest? |
| **Pipeline** | Catalogue-access requests; Fellowship-interest registrations; inbound DMs from target segments | The real leading indicators |

**Targets to set with the team:** a realistic month-1 follower floor, a newsletter signup goal, and a "first 10 qualified Fellow-interest registrations" milestone by end of August.

## 11. Workflow notes

- **Batch by anchor.** Write Monday's anchor + Wednesday's secondary at the start of each week; the other 7 slots are re-cuts.
- **One week ahead, minimum.** Draft and schedule by the prior Friday so nothing ships reactively.
- **Repurpose ladder:** LinkedIn anchor → X thread → IG carousel → quote card → X one-liner. Same idea, five shapes.
- **Idea drops need clearance** (see §6) — keep a vetted, de-identified queue so a drop is never blocked.
- **Could become a living page:** if useful, this can be rendered as an internal `brand/calendar.html` matching the `foundations.html` style. Say the word.
