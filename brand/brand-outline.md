# House of Ideas — Brand Outline

A foundational brand document covering positioning, voice, visual direction, and external narrative. Intended for internal alignment, designer briefs, and investor/partner materials.

> **This is the strategic *why*.** For the operational *how* — live type specimens, colour tokens, components, and do/don't rendered with the site's own CSS — see the living style guide at [`foundations.html`](../foundations.html).

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**One line:** *House of Ideas is where India's sharpest minds build the ventures the country actually needs.*

**Three words:** Considered. Selective. Builder.

**The promise:** We bring the idea, the capital, the plan, and the mentorship. The Fellow brings the execution.

---

## 2. Positioning

**Category:** Venture studio (not a fund, not an incubator, not an accelerator).

**Positioning statement:**
> For ambitious students from India's premier institutions who want to build instead of placing, House of Ideas is the venture studio that delivers researched ideas, real capital, and operational scaffolding — so that the Fellow's only job is to execute. Unlike traditional funds and accelerators, we do the upstream work ourselves and select the operator afterwards.

**Reference brands (for triangulation, not imitation):**
- **Atomic / eFounders / Expa** — the studio operating model
- **McKinsey / Goldman / Bain** — the elitist talent filter as identity
- **The New Yorker / The Economist** — editorial gravitas in voice and design
- **Stripe / Linear** — modern restraint and precision in execution

---

## 3. Brand Pillars

Four ideas that every piece of communication should reinforce:

1. **Conviction before capital.** We do the research first. Ideas are validated before a Fellow ever sees them. Capital follows conviction, not the other way around.
2. **The filter is the point.** We work only with students from IITs, NITs, and a defined set of India's top institutions. This is deliberate, not arbitrary.
3. **We build, we don't bet.** We are not passive investors. HoI runs operations. The Fellow drives product and customer thinking.
4. **A real path, not a pitch deck.** Every venture comes with a phased roadmap. The Fellow inherits structure, not a blank page.

---

## 4. Brand Personality

**Archetype blend:** *The Sage* (knowledge, considered judgment) crossed with *The Creator* (the urge to build something real).

**Personality attributes:**
| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Considered | Hype-driven |
| Selective | Exclusive-for-its-own-sake |
| Direct | Cold |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Editorial | Corporate |
| Builder | Hustler |
| Patient | Slow |

**Core tension to hold:** Elitist filter, generous spirit. The standards are high, but the relationship with Fellows is mentor-like, not transactional.

---

## 5. Voice & Tone

### Voice principles
- **Plain over puffed.** No "synergistic" anything. No "10x", no "disruption", no "ecosystem-builder".
- **Specific over sweeping.** "We bring the idea, the capital, the plan, and the mentorship" beats "We provide world-class funding".
- **Calm over loud.** The brand carries authority. It doesn't need to shout.
- **First-person plural, sparingly.** "We" when describing the studio; otherwise the brand speaks in declarative third person.

### Tonal range
- **Public-facing (site, social):** Editorial, declarative, slightly literary.
- **Fellow-facing (applications, mentoring):** Warm, direct, mentor-like.
- **Investor/partner-facing:** Crisp, evidence-led, numbers up front.

### Voice in action
| Off-brand | On-brand |
|---|---|
| "Unleash your potential with HoI!" | "Build something real. With structure, capital, and people who've done it." |
| "We empower student entrepreneurs to disrupt industries." | "We fund ventures. Students from India's top institutions run them." |
| "Apply now to join our exclusive network!" | "Applications are reviewed by the HoI team. Selection is rigorous." |

---

## 6. Naming System

- **The company:** House of Ideas. Abbreviated **HoI** (capital H, lowercase o, capital I) — never "HOI", never "Hoi".
- **The operator:** **Fellow** (capitalised as a proper noun within HoI contexts). Plural: **Fellows**. The program is the **Founders Fellowship**.
- **The portfolio company:** **venture** (lowercase) or named directly. Avoid "startup" in formal copy — too generic.
- **The pipeline:** **Idea** (capitalised when referring to a researched HoI Idea in the catalogue, lowercase in general use).
- **The product line currently in scope:** **Founders**. (The deferred talent product is **Workforce** — do not surface externally yet.)

## 7. Audience Segments & Messaging

### A. Prospective Fellows (primary)
*IIT/NIT students in their 2nd–4th year, intellectually restless, weighing placement vs. building.*

**What they want to hear:** *You don't have to choose between a safe career and building. We've removed the parts that usually break student founders — the blank page, the lack of capital, the loneliness.*

**Hooks:**
- Stipend during college + path to full-time post-graduation
- Real capital, real mentorship, real operating support
- The venture is already de-risked at the idea and roadmap level

### B. Investors & LPs
*Angels, family offices, and institutional partners who back the studio.*

**What they want to hear:** *A repeatable engine for producing fundable ventures, with a structural talent advantage no fund can replicate.*

**Hooks:**
- Studio model: pre-validated ideas, lower failure rate vs. cold founder pitches
- Talent filter: institutional moat
- HoI retains operational control + IP ownership

### C. Domain experts, mentors, advisors
*Operators and academics HoI partners with for research and mentorship.*

**What they want to hear:** *Serious, well-funded effort to build the next generation of Indian operators — and a chance to leave a fingerprint on the ones who matter.*

### D. Premier institutions (universities)
*IIT/NIT placement cells, entrepreneurship cells, faculty.*

**What they want to hear:** *A legitimate, structured alternative to placement that doesn't pull students away from their degree.*

## 9. Visual Direction (recommended)

The visual identity should feel like a **modern editorial institution** — closer to *The Economist* and *Stripe* than to a typical Indian tech startup. Reserved, confident, intellectually serious. The brand should look like it knows things.

### 9.1 Logo

**Recommendation: wordmark-led identity.** The brand *is* the name. No pictorial mark for the primary logo.

- **Primary lockup:** "House of Ideas" set in a refined serif, generous letter-spacing, single weight. Editorial nameplate energy — think masthead, not startup.
- **Secondary mark:** A monogram "**HoI**" for favicons, app icons, social avatars, and tight spaces. Set in the same serif. Possibly enclosed in a thin rule-line box (institutional, like a publisher's seal) — but the wordmark itself stays clean.
- **Avoid:** abstract geometric marks, lightbulbs, brains, gradients, isometric illustrations, anything that looks like an app icon competing on a Play Store grid.

### 9.2 Color Palette

A restrained palette built around **ink, paper, and one signal colour**.

| Role | Colour | Hex (starting point) | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary — Ink | Near-black, slightly warm | `#0E0E0C` | Body text, headlines, primary surfaces |
| Primary — Paper | Off-white, warm | `#F6F1E8` | Backgrounds, large surfaces |
| Accent — Signal | Deep ochre / aged gold | `#B8862F` | Single accent: links, CTAs, key highlights only |
| Neutral 1 | Stone grey | `#6B6660` | Captions, metadata, secondary text |
| Neutral 2 | Rule line | `#D9D2C5` | Dividers, table rules, borders |

**Rationale:** Ink + paper + a single warm metallic conveys gravitas without being corporate-cold. The ochre nods at Indian heritage (saffron, brass, paper) without resorting to the literal saffron-white-green palette. The result feels institutional and rooted, not nationalist.

**Rules:**
- The accent colour appears *sparingly*. If two ochre elements are on a screen, one is too many.
- Never use the accent for body text.
- No gradients. No drop shadows beyond the most minimal elevation. No glassmorphism.

### 9.3 Typography

A two-typeface system: a confident serif for display, a clean grotesque sans for body.

- **Display / Headlines:** A literary serif with weight and personality. *Recommended families:* **GT Sectra**, **Tiempos Headline**, or **Söhne Breit** (if a more geometric serif is preferred). Free alternative: **Source Serif 4**.
- **Body / UI:** A modern grotesque sans. *Recommended families:* **Söhne**, **Inter**, or **Untitled Sans**. Free alternative: **Inter** (Google Fonts).
- **Mono (rare, for data/tables):** **JetBrains Mono** or **IBM Plex Mono**.

**Type rules:**
- Headlines: tight tracking, generous line-height, no all-caps for long copy.
- Body: 16–18px base, line-height 1.5–1.65.
- Italics in the serif carry meaning — use them for editorial emphasis, not for entire paragraphs.

### 9.4 Imagery & Photography

- **Portraits of Fellows in working contexts** — not posed, not stock. Real desks, real whiteboards, real coffee. Shot in available light. Treated in muted colour or duotone (paper + ink).
- **Documentary, not aspirational.** No suits, no rooftop fist-bumps, no diverse-team-pointing-at-laptop tropes.
- **Avoid illustration entirely** for v1. No doodles, no hand-drawn flourishes, no isometric vector art. The brand earns trust by *not* decorating itself.
- **Diagrams** (for roadmaps, frameworks) drawn in pure black-on-paper line work. Like a textbook, not a SaaS landing page.

### 9.5 Layout & Web

- **Single-column, generous margins.** The site reads like a publication, not a product page.
- **Wide leading, long line-length on prose pages.** It should feel like reading something serious.
- **Hairline rules** (1px, neutral 2) as the primary divider — not coloured boxes.
- **No carousels, no auto-playing video, no chat bubbles.** Stillness is part of the brand.
- **Mobile:** the same restraint scales down. No hamburger menu animations or splash screens.

### 9.6 Motion

If motion is used at all: slow, considered, eased. Fades and minor positional shifts only. Nothing bounces. Nothing spins. The brand doesn't perform.

## 10. Brand Do's and Don'ts

**Do**
- Lead with specificity (numbers, names, mechanisms).
- Let white space and silence do work.
- Treat the Fellow as the protagonist of every external story.
- Reference the upstream research as proof of seriousness.

**Don't**
- Use startup clichés ("disrupt", "unicorn", "10x", "rockstar", "ninja").
- Promise outcomes ("guaranteed exit", "first unicorn from our batch").
- Use the IIT/NIT filter to mock other institutions — the filter is positioning, not snobbery.
- Over-explain the model in every piece of copy. Trust the reader.
- Use saffron-white-green together. Heritage cues should be subtle.

## 11. Open Questions (to resolve before lockdown)

- **Tagline lockup:** is "Build something real" strong enough as a public-facing tagline, or do we want something more institutional ("A house for India's builders")?
- **Logo wordmark:** custom-drawn serif or off-the-shelf? Custom adds 4–6 weeks but is the highest-leverage spend.
- **Photography commission:** who shoots the first Fellow cohort? This sets the visual canon for years.
- **Domain & social handles:** confirm `houseofideas.in` / `.co` / `.vc` and reserve handles before any external launch.
- **The Workforce sub-brand:** when (and how) does it surface? Same wordmark or distinct?
