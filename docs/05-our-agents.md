# House of Ideas — Our Agents

---

## The Operational Intelligence Layer

Running a startup is not just about building the product. It is about staying compliant, keeping investors informed, hiring the right people, managing documents, and handling hundreds of small but critical tasks — consistently, without dropping anything.

Most startups hire a team to do this. We built agents instead.

HoI's agents are purpose-built to handle the repetitive, time-consuming operational work that pulls founders away from what matters. A Fellow running a HoI venture does not need a 10-person ops team. They have agents.

---

## Idea Discovery Agent

The Idea Discovery Agent is where every HoI venture begins.

It continuously scans domains, analyses trends, maps market gaps, and surfaces startup ideas with genuine potential. For each idea it identifies, it produces a structured brief — the problem, the market, the execution approach, and why now.

This agent does not just list ideas. It scores them, stress-tests them against known failure patterns, and flags the ones worth building. The output feeds directly into HoI's venture pipeline.

**What it handles:**
- Domain trend analysis and opportunity mapping
- Idea generation and structured evaluation
- Execution roadmap scaffolding
- Competitive landscape snapshots

---

## Documentation Agent

Every venture runs on documents — and most founders hate writing them.

The Documentation Agent handles the full lifecycle of operational documentation. From internal SOPs to product specifications, from meeting notes to progress reports, it drafts, formats, and maintains documents so that nothing important goes unrecorded and nothing needs to be written twice.

**What it handles:**
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for recurring workflows
- Product requirement documents and technical specs
- Meeting notes and action item logs
- Monthly and quarterly progress reports
- Investor update drafts

---

## Compliance Agent

Startups in India operate inside a maze of regulatory requirements. Missing a filing or a deadline has consequences that are disproportionate to the oversight.

The Compliance Agent tracks every regulatory obligation relevant to the venture — from company registration to GST filings, from labour law requirements to sector-specific rules. It sends reminders, prepares checklists, and flags anything that needs founder or legal attention.

**What it handles:**
- MCA filings and company secretarial reminders
- GST, TDS, and tax compliance calendars
- Labour law obligations (PF, ESIC, shops act)
- Sector-specific regulatory tracking
- Deadline alerts and compliance status summaries

---

## HR Agent

Hiring is one of the highest-leverage things a founder does. It is also one of the most process-heavy.

The HR Agent takes the administrative load off the founder's plate — from drafting job descriptions to managing candidate pipelines, from sending offer letters to running structured onboarding. It does not replace the founder's judgment on who to hire. It removes every obstacle between that judgment and action.

**What it handles:**
- Job description drafting and posting
- Candidate pipeline tracking
- Interview scheduling and coordination
- Offer letter generation
- Onboarding checklists and day-one documentation
- Payroll reminders and leave tracking

---

## Finance Agent

A founder who does not know their runway, burn rate, and unit economics is flying blind.

The Finance Agent keeps the venture's financial picture clear and current — without requiring a full-time finance hire. It processes transactions, tracks expenses by category, generates MIS reports, and surfaces the numbers founders need to make decisions.

**What it handles:**
- Expense tracking and categorisation
- Invoicing and payment reminders
- Monthly MIS reports
- Runway and burn rate calculations
- Cash flow summaries

---

## Legal Agent

Legal work is expensive, slow, and unavoidable. Most early-stage founders either overpay for it or skip it — both are mistakes.

The Legal Agent handles the drafting and review of standard legal documents that every startup needs — NDAs, vendor agreements, service contracts, and equity term sheet scaffolding. It flags clauses that need human legal review and ensures that nothing routine ever waits on a lawyer.

**What it handles:**
- NDA and confidentiality agreement drafts
- Vendor and service contract templates
- Equity term sheet scaffolding
- IP assignment agreement drafts
- Contract clause flagging and review notes

---

## Investor Relations Agent

Investors need to hear from you regularly. Most founders either under-communicate or send updates that say very little.

The Investor Relations Agent handles the rhythm of investor communication — drafting monthly updates, maintaining the data room, tracking investor asks, and preparing follow-up correspondence. It ensures that every investor stays informed and that the venture never goes dark.

**What it handles:**
- Monthly investor update drafts
- Data room organisation and maintenance
- Investor query tracking and follow-up drafts
- Cap table summary generation
- Fundraise process documentation

---

## Customer Support Agent

The first version of customer support at every startup is a founder answering emails. That does not scale.

The Customer Support Agent handles tier-1 queries — FAQs, onboarding questions, standard troubleshooting, and routine requests. It routes complex issues to the right person and ensures that no customer message goes unanswered. The founder only sees what genuinely needs them.

**What it handles:**
- FAQ response management
- Onboarding and setup query handling
- Escalation routing to the right team member
- Response time tracking
- Support ticket categorisation and logging

---

## Research Agent

Good decisions need good information. Research takes time that founders do not have.

The Research Agent runs structured research on demand — market sizing, competitor analysis, industry trend reports, customer segment profiles, and technology landscape briefs. Every output is structured for decision-making, not just reading.

**What it handles:**
- Market sizing and TAM/SAM/SOM estimates
- Competitor landscape analysis
- Industry and sector trend briefs
- Customer segment profiling
- Technology and tool evaluation reports
