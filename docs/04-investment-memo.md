# House of Ideas — Investment Memo

*Confidential — For Partners and Advisors*

---

## Overview

House of Ideas (HoI) is a venture studio based in India. We research, validate, and fund high-potential ideas — then recruit exceptional students from India's premier institutions to build them.

We are not a traditional venture fund. We do not wait for founders to pitch us. We source the ideas, build the execution roadmap, deploy the capital, and find the right person to run each venture.

---

## The Opportunity

India's premier engineering institutions — IITs, NITs, and a defined set of top colleges — produce some of the most capable young talent in the world. The gap is not talent. The gap is access to structured, funded, mentored opportunities to build.

At the same time, the Indian market has significant whitespace across multiple high-growth domains: AI, HealthTech, EdTech, AgriTech, FinTech, ClimaTech, and more. Many ideas that are obvious to research are never executed because no one has the combination of capital, structure, and the right people in place simultaneously.

HoI is designed to close both gaps at once.

---

## The Model

### How HoI Creates Value

**1. Idea Origination**
HoI runs a continuous research operation across 10 active domains. Ideas are validated against a six-point framework (problem clarity, market size, timing, execution path, competitive landscape, India fit) before they are listed on the platform or assigned to a Fellow.

**2. Capital Deployment**
HoI deploys capital from Angel to VC scale per venture, depending on the stage and scope. Capital is deployed in phases tied to execution milestones, not upfront in a single tranche.

**3. Talent Acquisition**
The HoI platform lists available ventures. Students from IIT/NIT/premier institutions apply to build them. Selection is rigorous. We look for execution mindset, not just academic performance.

**4. Operational Infrastructure**
HoI runs operations for each venture while the Fellow is in college. This includes legal, finance, vendor management, and basic GTM — so the Fellow can focus entirely on product and customer development.

**5. Mentorship and Roadmap**
Every venture comes with a step-by-step execution roadmap built by the HoI team. Fellows are not left to figure out the path — they are guided through it with active mentorship from HoI partners and domain advisors.

---

## IP and Ownership Structure

All intellectual property developed within HoI-backed ventures is owned by House of Ideas. This is the foundation of the studio model.

IP ownership gives HoI:
- Continuity — ventures survive Fellow transitions
- Control — HoI can make directional decisions without founder conflict
- Portfolio value — IP assets are held at the studio level and appreciate with each venture's progress

Fellows are compensated through:
- A monthly stipend during the fellowship (college period)
- A pre-agreed equity stake, activated if and when the Fellow returns post-graduation to run the venture full-time

---

## Portfolio Construction

HoI targets a portfolio of 8–12 active ventures at any given time across its defined domain framework.

Each venture is sized based on its stage and capital requirements. Follow-on capital is evaluated on a per-venture basis as milestones are hit.

**Target portfolio mix (indicative):**
- 40% AI & Intelligent Systems
- 20% EdTech / HealthTech
- 20% FinTech / Enterprise SaaS
- 20% Emerging domains (AgriTech, ClimaTech, GovTech, LegalTech)

---

## Risk Factors and Mitigants

| Risk | Mitigant |
|---|---|
| Fellow drops out mid-build | HoI owns IP and runs operations; venture continues with a new Fellow |
| Idea fails market validation | Phased capital deployment limits downside; validated before listing |
| Talent quality variance | Strict institutional filter and multi-stage selection process |
| Operational load of running multiple ventures | HoI team is structured as a studio operator, not a passive investor |
| Equity dilution at post-college return | Equity framework agreed upfront and documented in Fellowship Agreement |

---

## The HoI Advantage

**Why this works in India specifically:**

- IIT/NIT students are globally competitive but locally under-resourced for early-stage building
- India's regulatory environment rewards early movers in several priority domains (AI, HealthTech, AgriTech)
- The studio model is proven globally (Atomic, eFounders, Expa) but not yet applied at scale to the Indian institutional talent pool
- The HoI brand, once established, becomes self-reinforcing: the best students want the HoI association, which raises the talent bar further
