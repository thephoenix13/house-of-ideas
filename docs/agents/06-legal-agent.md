# Legal Agent

---

## What It Does

The Legal Agent handles the drafting and first-pass review of standard legal documents that every startup encounters — NDAs, vendor contracts, service agreements, IP assignments, and equity term sheet scaffolding. It ensures that routine legal work never waits on a lawyer, and that nothing important gets signed without review.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Legal work at early-stage startups falls into two categories: standard documents that follow well-established templates, and complex situations that genuinely require a lawyer.

Most founders conflate the two. They either skip legal review entirely (a risk) or send every standard document to a lawyer at full billing rates (expensive and slow). The Legal Agent handles the first category completely and hands off the second with a clear brief — so that legal spend is reserved for situations that actually need it.

---

## How It Works

The agent maintains a library of standard legal templates calibrated for Indian startups. When a legal document is needed, the founder describes the context — who the counterparty is, what the agreement covers, what the key terms are — and the agent generates a draft.

Every draft includes a review note flagging clauses that deviate from standard terms or that carry non-trivial risk. The founder can accept, edit, or send to legal counsel with the agent's risk summary already attached.

---

## What It Handles

- NDA and confidentiality agreement drafts (mutual and one-way)
- Vendor and supplier agreement templates
- Service and consulting contract drafts
- Employment agreement templates
- IP assignment agreement drafts
- Equity term sheet scaffolding
- Contract clause flagging and risk notes
- Reminder tracking for contract renewals and expiries

---

## Output

A drafted legal document ready for review, with a structured risk note highlighting any clauses that warrant legal attention. Routine agreements are turned around quickly; complex situations are escalated with context already prepared.
