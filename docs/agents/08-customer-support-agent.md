# Customer Support Agent

---

## What It Does

The Customer Support Agent handles the first layer of customer communication — answering common queries, managing onboarding questions, routing complex issues, and ensuring that no customer message goes unanswered — so that founders only see what genuinely needs their attention.

---

## The Problem It Solves

The first version of customer support at every startup is a founder answering emails. That is the right call at the very beginning — founders need to hear directly from customers. But it does not scale, and it does not need to be the founder handling every repeat question about pricing, setup, or account access.

Unanswered support queries churn customers. Slow responses damage trust. The Customer Support Agent ensures that every customer gets a timely, accurate response to routine queries, while the founder stays focused on the conversations that are actually signal.

---

## How It Works

The agent is trained on the venture's product documentation, FAQs, and known issue resolutions. It handles incoming queries by matching them to known answers or workflows. When a query falls outside its knowledge — a novel bug, a billing dispute, a partnership inquiry — it routes to the right person with context already attached.

It tracks every interaction, maintains response time metrics, and surfaces patterns in customer queries that indicate product gaps or documentation failures.

---

## What It Handles

- FAQ and common query responses
- Product onboarding and setup guidance
- Account and access management queries
- Known issue acknowledgements and resolution updates
- Escalation routing with full context attached
- Response time tracking and SLA monitoring
- Support ticket categorisation and logging
- Pattern analysis across support queries

---

## Output

Every customer query receives a timely response. The founder receives a weekly summary of support volume, common issues, escalations, and any patterns worth addressing in the product or documentation.
