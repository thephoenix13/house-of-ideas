# Finance Agent

---

## What It Does

The Finance Agent keeps the venture's financial picture clear, current, and decision-ready — without requiring a full-time finance hire. It processes transactions, tracks expenses, generates reports, and surfaces the numbers founders need to understand how the business is running.

---

## The Problem It Solves

A founder who does not know their runway, burn rate, and unit economics is flying blind. Financial visibility is not optional — it is the difference between catching a problem early and discovering it too late.

At the same time, maintaining books, reconciling accounts, generating MIS reports, and chasing invoices is work that does not require a founder's attention. It requires consistency, accuracy, and follow-through. The Finance Agent provides all three.

---

## How It Works

The agent connects to the venture's financial data — bank statements, expense records, invoices — and maintains a continuously updated picture of the venture's financial position. It categorises transactions, tracks outstanding receivables, flags anomalies, and generates structured reports on a defined schedule.

For decisions that require judgment — a large capital allocation, a pricing change, an investment round — it surfaces the data the founder needs rather than making the call itself.

---

## What It Handles

- Transaction categorisation and bookkeeping
- Expense tracking by category and team
- Invoice generation and payment reminders
- Accounts receivable and payable tracking
- Monthly MIS reports (P&L, cash flow, balance sheet summary)
- Runway and burn rate calculations
- Budget vs. actuals tracking
- Financial data packaging for investor reviews

---

## Output

Monthly MIS reports, a live runway dashboard, expense summaries by category, and outstanding invoice tracking. The founder always has an accurate, up-to-date picture of the venture's financial health.
