# House of Ideas — Founders Fellowship Brief

---

## What Is the Founders Fellowship?

The HoI Founders Fellowship is a structured program that places exceptional students from India's premier institutions at the centre of real, funded ventures — while they're still in college.

This is not an internship. This is not a hackathon. This is not a case competition.

Fellows are embedded in an active venture, working on a real problem, with real capital behind it, and a real path to equity when they graduate.

---

## Who Is This For?

The Fellowship is open to students currently enrolled at:

- Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs)
- National Institutes of Technology (NITs)
- Other premier institutions as defined by HoI

We look for students who are:

- Intellectually curious and commercially aware
- Capable of independent thinking under ambiguity
- Genuinely interested in building — not just in having built
- In their second year or above (preferred — enough runway before graduation)

We do not require prior startup experience. We do not filter by branch or discipline. We filter for mindset.

---

## Two Ways to Apply

### Track A — Build an HoI Idea
Explore the ideas listed on the HoI platform. Each idea comes with a research brief, a competitive landscape summary, and a phased execution roadmap. If one resonates, apply to take it forward as a Fellow.

### Track B — Bring Your Own Problem
If you're already working on a problem — but limited by structure, capital, or scale — you can apply to bring your venture into the HoI portfolio. HoI will evaluate fit, and if selected, will co-shape the direction and provide the resources needed to move forward.

In both tracks, IP is held by House of Ideas. This ensures continuity of the venture regardless of who builds it at any given stage.

---

## What Fellows Receive

**During College**

- Monthly stipend (amount communicated at offer stage)
- Direct access to the HoI team for mentoring and decision support
- A step-by-step execution roadmap for the venture
- Operational infrastructure — HoI handles the business operations so Fellows can focus on building
- Exposure to real capital deployment, vendor management, and product decisions
- Association with the HoI portfolio and network

**After College**

- The option to return to the venture full-time
- A pre-agreed equity stake, formalised at the point of selection
- A defined role in the venture's next phase of growth

---

## What the Fellowship Is Not

- It is not a part-time project or a side commitment. Fellows are expected to treat this as their primary building endeavour during the fellowship period.
- It is not a shortcut to a job. It is a path to building something.
- It is not open-ended. Fellows have a defined scope and roadmap to execute against.

---

## The Selection Process

**Stage 1 — Application**
A structured written application. We want to understand how you think, not just what you've done. Questions will be specific to the idea or track you've applied for.

**Stage 2 — Problem Deep-Dive**
A focused conversation with the HoI team. We'll go deep on the idea, the market, your thinking, and your approach. No presentations required.

**Stage 3 — Decision**
HoI communicates a go/no-go within a defined window. If selected, you receive a Fellowship Agreement covering stipend terms, IP assignment, equity framework, and post-college option.

---

## The Post-College Equity Option

When a Fellow graduates, they have the right — not the obligation — to return to the venture as a full-time operator.

The equity stake is:
- Agreed upon and documented at the time of selection
- Held in reserve for the Fellow until graduation
- Activated upon the Fellow's formal return to the venture

If a Fellow chooses not to return, HoI continues the venture with a new operator. The Fellow's equity option lapses per the terms of the Fellowship Agreement.

---

## Why This Model Works

For the Fellow, the proposition is straightforward:

You get to build something real during college — with money, mentorship, a plan, and a team behind you. When you graduate, instead of walking into a placement, you can walk back into a venture where you already have history, context, and equity waiting.

For HoI, the model ensures that every venture in the portfolio is run by someone who earned the right to be there — not someone who happened to have an idea in the shower.

---

## A Note on the IIT/NIT Filter

This is a deliberate choice, not an oversight.

The Founders Fellowship is built around a specific talent standard. Working only with students from India's top institutions is how HoI maintains that standard consistently across every venture in the portfolio.

This filter is not a judgement on talent elsewhere. It is a positioning decision — one that allows HoI to build a recognisable, trusted brand around the quality of who it works with.
