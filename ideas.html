01 — How an idea makes the catalogue

Six dimensions. Every idea is tested against all of them.

Ideas surface continuously from our research operation. Only those that pass a structured six-dimension review reach the catalogue — and only the top scores go into a full brief.

01

Problem clarity

Is the pain point specific and verifiable, or vague and aspirational?

02

Market size

Is there a defensible TAM, or is this a niche dressed up as an opportunity?

03

Timing

Why now? What has changed in regulation, technology, or behaviour?

04

Execution path

Can a small team make meaningful progress in six months with early capital?

05

Competitive landscape

Who else is here, and is there a real gap, or a crowded space?

06

India fit

Does this work inside the Indian regulatory and cultural context, not just in theory?

02 — Request a full brief

The cards show the surface. The brief goes six layers deep.

Each full brief includes the research hypothesis, addressable market with sizing, competitive landscape, why-now analysis, India-specific execution factors, and a phased six-month roadmap — the kind of detail we would not publish openly while ideas are still pre-allocation.
